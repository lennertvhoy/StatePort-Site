What a future release will include

When StatePort is ready to make a public release, this page should bind its claims to exact, independently checkable material rather than a vague “available now” badge.

  • A public source boundary and an explicit license decision.
  • A versioned release with notes tied to that source.
  • Verified artifacts or installers, with appropriate checksums where applicable.
  • Platform support and installation guidance that has been tested on the stated environment.
  • Clear separation between implemented, locally validated, remotely accepted, and human-accepted release facts.

Why no premature download?

StatePort treats evidence as part of a reliable lifecycle. That makes it better to state “not published” than to offer a command that depends on private source, an unverified environment, or an unfinished release decision.
