# LinkedIn post draft

The work does not end when the conversation does.

Most things worth keeping—a decision, an approval, a plan, a handover—need a
place that survives a new session, a different tool, or a change of host.

That is the question behind StatePort: an application-first Stateware project
where conversation is useful, but the application keeps the work, its history,
and its ownership.

The first public site is now live:

https://lennertvhoy.github.io/StatePort-Site/

It includes a short product preview, the Stateware paper, field notes on the
model, and a release ledger that keeps availability in one honest place.

I am still preparing the public software release. For now, I would love to
hear from people building tools around long-lived work: what do you wish still
made sense after the conversation ends?
