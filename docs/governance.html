Reading guide This page explores the model. Availability lives in the release ledger.

The action lifecycle

intent → prepared proposal and preconditions → exact grant or approval → bounded execution → transactional apply → validation result → receipt → optional independent or human acceptance

The order matters. A model can propose without gaining authority. An applied change can fail validation. A locally validated result can remain pending independent review. Keeping those facts separate makes failure visible instead of hiding it behind “done.”

Authority should be specific

Routine work may fit inside a bounded, revocable standing grant. Destructive, expensive, or external side effects should stop for a fresh decision. The exact policy depends on the application, but neither a template nor an assistant gets to widen its own authority.

Receipts help a person ask the right questions

  • What was requested and against which identity?
  • Who or what authorized it?
  • What did the system actually try to do?
  • What validation ran, and what did it report?
  • Which facts remain pending, unavailable, or rejected?

A receipt is a durable claim with evidence, not a substitute for judgement. A well-governed bad decision is still a bad decision; the value is that it is visible, attributable, and reviewable.

Degraded modes are part of honesty

Failing closed is a policy choice, not a reason to pretend an application is useless. Read-only, blocked, unavailable, and degraded states can be useful as long as the interface says what changed, why, and what a person can do next.
