Glossary

Stateware

Software whose durable, inspectable state and governed lifecycle contracts form the application boundary while agents and models remain replaceable processors.

StateSpec

The portable, repository-native specification for durable facts, ownership, requested capabilities, lifecycle rules, and validation expectations. StateDD is a legacy compatibility name, not the current public term.

Canonical state

The declared source of truth for a specific concern. It is not a synonym for every file, message, cache, or external system record.

Instance

A particular durable application copy with its own identity, private state, grants, and lifecycle history.

Receipt

A structured claim about a transition and its evidence. It can be reviewed and verified; it does not replace judgement or make an external system reversible.

Frequently asked questions

Where can I check availability?

The release ledger is the source for public availability.

Is a conversation the application’s truth?

No. A conversation can be a durable operational record, but a domain fact needs an explicit owner and source of truth.

Does portability mean identical behavior everywhere?

No. It means an application can preserve its declared boundary while hosts report their actual capabilities and limitations.

Does a receipt prove a decision was good?

No. It makes the decision and its outcome easier to attribute and inspect. Human judgement remains necessary.

Where should I begin?

Read the foundations page, then the model and lifecycle. The first-application tutorial turns those concepts into a small design exercise.
